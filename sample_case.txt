CASE TITLE: Smith v. Johnson

FACTS:
Plaintiff John Smith was driving his vehicle on Main Street when Defendant Jane Johnson, who was texting on her phone, ran a red light and collided with Plaintiff's vehicle. Plaintiff suffered injuries including a broken leg and incurred medical expenses of $50,000. Defendant admits to texting but claims the accident was unavoidable due to sudden brake failure.

ISSUES:
1. Was Defendant negligent in operating her vehicle while texting?
2. Did Defendant's negligence cause Plaintiff's injuries?
3. What damages, if any, is Plaintiff entitled to?

HOLDING:
The court finds Defendant negligent for texting while driving, which was the proximate cause of the accident. Plaintiff is awarded $50,000 in damages plus costs.
